Kannur

"The Crown of Kerala"

Located on the Malabar Coast of Kerala, Kannur (fondly known as Cannanore), is a beautiful city bundled with a plethora of sightseeing options like beaches, monuments, ancient temples and picnic spots.The deep footprints of the Dutch, the Portuguese, the British and the Mysore Sultanate are still imbibed into the town and can be witnessed all around.

Direction : MapView

1. Parassinikadavu Snake Park

13 km from city center

Regarded as one of the finest snake sanctuaries of Asia, the Parassinikadavu Snake Park is the pride of the south. It is one of the most prominent reptile preservation centres in the country and is the only one in Kerala.

2. Payyambalam Beach

2 km from city center

The impeccably unspoiled Payyabalam beach is adorned with sparkling pearls of golden sand and foamy white waves of the Arabian Sea.Palm trees and casuarinas embellish the shores like an alluring garland in various shades of green.

3. Kavvayi Islands

5 km from city center

Recorded in the travel writings of scholars and explorers Ibn Battuta and Marco Polo, Kavil Pattanam, now called Kavvayi, has been recreated into a beautiful island.The name Kavil Pattanam was changed to Kavvayi by Sir William Hogan.

4. Kannur Lighthouse

2 km from city center

Being Kerala's first lighthouse ever and Kannur's only one, the Kannur Lighthouse is a pretty big deal.Standing tall at the height of 75 feet, this lighthouse was first built in the early months of 1903.
