Idukki

"Spice Garden of Kerala"

Idukki, a landlocked district of Kerala, is one of the most nature-rich areas in Kerala with more than half of the area covered with forest. This rugged hill resort is known for its wildlife sanctuaries, beautiful bungalows, tea factories, rubber plantations and forests.Anamudi, which is the highest peak in India, south of the Himalayas, is also located in Idukki.

Direction : MapView

1. Idukki Arch Dam

22 km from city center

Built across the Kuravan and Kurathi hills at a height of about 550 feet, the Idukki Arch Dam is an architectural genius, for the rugged terrain along which it is built. The natural beauty surrounding the dam is breath-taking.

2. Periyar National Park

32 km from city center

Famous for its Tiger reserves and thick vegetation, the Periyar National Park is a perfect place for nature buffs and wildlife lovers.However, the best way to explore this natural habitat is by safaris either on jeep or elephants.

3. Anamudi Peak

52 km from city center

Often called the pride of Kerala, Anamudi or Anai Mudi Peak in Munnar stands at a magnificent height of 2695 metres above sea level.It is also famous for the Neela Kurinji flower which is known to blossom only once every 12 years.

4. Eravikulam National Park

13 km from city center

Located a 45 - minute drive away from Munnar, the Eravikulam National Park houses a rich and diverse variety of flora and fauna and is a UNESCO world heritage site. Also known as Rajamalai Wildlife Sanctuary.
