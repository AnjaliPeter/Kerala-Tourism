Thiruvananthapuram

"The City of Lord Ananta"

The capital city of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram is an appealing blend of a strongly rooted heritage and a nostalgic colonial legacy.Proudly retaining its age-old cultural charm, a huge variety of sights including incredible museums, beautifully designed palaces, sacred temples and mesmerizing beaches, making the city one of the best tourist spots in South India.

Direction : MapView

1. Neyyar Dam & Wildlife Sanctuary

6 km from city center

Famous for its Lion and Deer Safari, Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary is a treat for nature enthusiasts. The park houses a crocodile farm and elephant rehabilitation centre. Ask for a guided tour of the sanctuary which is quite informative.

2. Zoological Park

18 km from city center

Hailed as the oldest zoo in India, this The Zoological Park is famous for its various bird species brought from Ethiopia & Australia. Visitors can also take a boat ride in the lake while exploring the nature''s beauty.

3. Science & Technology Museum

19 km from city center

Known for its tech on display, the Science and Technology Museum was founded in 1984. The museum draws a huge number of visitors into its chambers, especially the science enthusiasts.The institution is in the heart of Thiruvananthapuram city.

4. Napier Museum

18 km from city center

Renowned for its fine collection of sculptures, paintings and musical instruments, the Napier Museum serves as a prime attraction of the city. Located right in the heart of the city, it is also an architectural marvel which was established around 150 years ago.
